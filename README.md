# 📊 HR Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the major challenges faced by organizations because high employee turnover can increase recruitment costs, reduce productivity, and impact overall business performance.

This project explores the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset using Python to identify patterns and factors associated with employee turnover through Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing.
- Explore employee attrition patterns.
- Identify factors associated with employee turnover.
- Provide data-driven business recommendations.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Libraries

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## 📂 Dataset

- **Dataset:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance
- **Records:** 1,470 employees
- **Target Variable:** Attrition (Yes / No)

---

# 📈 Executive Summary

The exploratory analysis indicates that employee attrition is associated with several demographic and work-related factors.

Employees who work overtime tend to experience substantially higher attrition rates compared to those who do not.

Lower monthly income is also associated with higher employee turnover, suggesting that compensation may influence retention.

Employees who leave the company are generally younger and have fewer years of service, indicating that early-career employees are more likely to resign.

Certain job roles also exhibit higher attrition rates, highlighting the need for targeted retention strategies.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, several strategies can help reduce employee attrition:

- Reduce excessive overtime through workload balancing.
- Review compensation for lower-income employees.
- Develop career growth programs for high-risk job roles.
- Strengthen onboarding and mentoring for new employees.
- Monitor employees with short tenure using HR analytics.
